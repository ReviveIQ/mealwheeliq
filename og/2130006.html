🍲 Creamy Tomato Chicken over Cauliflower Mash

30 min · Easy · Mediterranean Comfort · 702 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Chicken Breast
  • 1 large head (about 2 lbs) Cauliflower
  • 1 can (15oz) Crushed Tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 3 cups Spinach
  • 0.5 cup Greek Yogurt
  • 2 oz Parmesan
  • 4 stalks Green Onions (green tops only)
  • 0.25 cup Fresh Parsley
  • 1 piece Lemon
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 4 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 tsp Fresh Ginger
  • 2 tbsp Garlic-infused olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prep: Cut cauliflower into florets and bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Slice chicken breasts horizontally into cutlets about 3/4-inch thick, pat dry, and season generously with paprika, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Thinly slice the green tops of the scallions and set aside.
  2. Boil cauliflower florets until completely tender and mashable, about 12 minutes, then drain thoroughly. Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp regular olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Sear chicken cutlets in the hot skillet until deeply golden and cooked through, about 4 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate, tent loosely with foil, and reduce skillet heat to medium.
  4. Build the sauce: Add garlic-infused olive oil and tomato paste to the same skillet and stir until paste darkens slightly, about 90 seconds. Pour in crushed tomatoes, grated fresh ginger, red pepper flakes, and Italian seasoning, stirring to combine. Simmer until slightly thickened, about 6 minutes, then reduce heat to low, fold in the spinach until just wilted, and remove from heat. Stir in Greek yogurt off the heat to prevent curdling.
  5. Mash the drained cauliflower with butter, remaining 2 tbsp regular olive oil, half the grated Parmesan, a squeeze of lemon juice, salt, and black pepper until smooth and creamy. Adjust consistency with a splash of the cauliflower cooking water if needed.
  6. Return chicken and any resting juices to the tomato sauce and gently warm together over low heat for 2 minutes. Taste and season the sauce with black pepper and a pinch of salt if needed.
  7. Spoon creamy cauliflower mash into shallow bowls, ladle tomato-spinach sauce and chicken over the top, and finish with remaining Parmesan, scallion greens, and fresh parsley. Serve immediately with lemon wedges on the side.
