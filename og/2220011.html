🍗 Spatchcock Chicken with Pickle Brine Pan Sauce & Roasted Potatoes

30 min · Medium · American · 516 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs Whole chicken
  • 0.75 lbs Potatoes
  • 0.25 cup Wickles Pickle Chips
  • 0.5 medium Red onion
  • 3 tbsp Butter
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 0.5 cup Chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp Hot sauce
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • 0.5 tsp Thyme

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F. Spatchcock the chicken by cutting out the backbone with kitchen shears, then pressing firmly on the breastbone to flatten. Pat completely dry with paper towels and season aggressively all over with salt, black pepper, paprika, and thyme.
  2. Toss potato wedges (cut into 1-inch pieces) with olive oil, salt, and garlic powder on a large rimmed sheet pan, spreading into a single layer. Nestle the flattened chicken skin-side up directly on top of the potatoes, pressing it flat.
  3. Roast at 450°F until chicken skin is deeply golden and crackling and an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the breast reads 160°F, about 30–35 minutes.
  4. Transfer chicken to a cutting board to rest 5 minutes. Toss potatoes on the pan and return to the oven for 5 more minutes to crisp.
  5. Meanwhile, melt butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add red onion and smashed garlic cloves, cooking until softened and lightly golden, about 3 minutes. Pour in chicken broth and 2 tablespoons of the Wickles pickle brine, then whisk in Dijon mustard and hot sauce. Simmer until reduced by half and slightly thickened, about 4 minutes.
  6. Remove pan from heat and stir in remaining 1 tablespoon butter until glossy. Taste and season with salt and black pepper.
  7. Carve the rested chicken into breast and thigh portions, arrange over the crisped potatoes, spoon the pickle-Dijon pan sauce generously over the top, and garnish with a few Wickles pickle chips for bright acidic crunch. Serve immediately straight from the pan.
