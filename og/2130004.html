🥩 Argentinian Chimichurri Pork Chops with Cauliflower Rice & Roasted Carrots

30 min · Medium · Argentinian · 131 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 3 pieces bone-in pork chops (about 10 oz each)
  • 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into 1-inch coins
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 0.25 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 4 stalks green onions, green tops only, thinly sliced
  • 0.5 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 8 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 piece lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss carrots and cherry tomatoes with 2 tbsp olive oil, paprika, cumin, salt, and black pepper on a rimmed baking sheet, spreading in a single layer. Roast until carrots are tender and caramelized at the edges, about 22–25 minutes.
  2. Whisk together the chimichurri while the carrots roast: combine parsley, cilantro, green onion tops, red pepper flakes, oregano, red wine vinegar, lemon zest, lemon juice, and 4 tbsp olive oil in a bowl until fully incorporated. Season with a pinch of salt and set aside — the chimichurri deepens in flavor as it rests.
  3. Pulse cauliflower florets in a food processor until broken into rice-sized granules, about 8–10 pulses. Heat garlic-infused olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat, add the cauliflower rice, and toss to coat. Cook, stirring occasionally, until tender and lightly golden, about 6–8 minutes. Season with salt, black pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Transfer to a bowl and keep warm.
  4. Pat pork chops thoroughly dry with paper towels and season both sides generously with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of cumin and paprika. Heat remaining 2 tbsp olive oil in the same skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  5. Sear pork chops undisturbed until deeply golden-brown on the first side, about 4 minutes. Flip and sear the second side until just cooked through and an instant-read thermometer reads 145°F, about 3–4 minutes more. Transfer to a cutting board and rest 3 minutes.
  6. Spoon a mound of cauliflower rice onto each plate, arrange the roasted carrots and cherry tomatoes alongside, and lean one seared pork chop against the base. Spoon chimichurri generously over the pork and vegetables, taste, adjust salt if needed, and serve immediately with remaining chimichurri on the side.
