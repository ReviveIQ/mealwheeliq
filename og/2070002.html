🥢 Korean Beef & Broccoli Jeyuk Bokkeum Bowl

30 min · Easy · Korean · 705 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 0.75 lbs Ground Beef
  • 2 cups Frozen Broccoli
  • 1 large Bell Pepper
  • 2 tbsp Gochujang
  • 2 tbsp Sesame Oil
  • 3 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1 medium Onion
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable Oil
  • 1 tsp Cornstarch
  • 2 cups cooked Rice or cooked white rice
  • 1 tsp Red Pepper Flakes
  • 1 tbsp Vinegar

Instructions

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, gochujang, honey, sesame oil, vinegar, and cornstarch in a small bowl until smooth; set the sauce aside. Thinly slice the bell pepper and onion, and mince the garlic.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until shimmering and just beginning to smoke. Add onion and bell pepper, tossing frequently until charred at the edges and softened, about 4 minutes.
  3. Push vegetables to one side and add ground beef in a single layer to the cleared side of the pan. Sear undisturbed until a deep brown crust forms on the bottom, about 3 minutes, then break apart and cook through, about 2 minutes more.
  4. Stir in garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until fragrant, about 45 seconds, then add frozen broccoli directly to the pan and toss everything together, cooking until broccoli is heated through and bright green, about 3 minutes.
  5. Pour the gochujang sauce over the pan contents and toss aggressively over high heat until every piece is lacquered and the sauce thickens and clings, about 2 minutes.
  6. Taste, season with salt and black pepper as needed, then serve over steamed white rice, drizzling any remaining pan sauce over the top.
