🍱 Japanese-Style Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowl with Sesame Broccoli & Edamame

30 min · Easy · Japanese · 714 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lbs salmon fillets
  • 1.5 cups dry white rice
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 1.5 cups frozen edamame (shelled)
  • 3 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce (tamari, gluten-free)
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1.5 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1.5 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 0.5 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 3 stalks scallion green tops, thinly sliced
  • 1.5 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook rice according to package directions using 3 cups water and a pinch of salt — this will run concurrently with the rest of cooking, about 18 minutes total.
  2. Whisk together tamari, mirin, sugar, grated ginger, and cornstarch in a small bowl until sugar dissolves — this is your teriyaki glaze. Set aside.
  3. Heat 1.5 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Pat salmon fillets dry, season with salt and black pepper, and sear skin-side up until deeply golden, about 3–4 minutes per side. Pour the teriyaki glaze over the salmon, reduce heat to medium, and cook 2 minutes more, spooning glaze over the top until it thickens and coats the fish in a glossy lacquer. Transfer to a plate.
  4. Wipe the skillet clean and return to medium-high heat. Add remaining 1.5 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil and sesame oil, then toss in broccoli florets and frozen edamame with red pepper flakes and a pinch of salt. Stir-fry until broccoli is bright green and tender-crisp and edamame is heated through, about 5–6 minutes.
  5. Fluff the cooked rice and divide evenly among 3 wide bowls. Top each with a salmon fillet and a generous scoop of the sesame broccoli and edamame. Spoon any remaining teriyaki glaze from the pan over the salmon, garnish with scallion greens and sesame seeds, taste for seasoning, and serve immediately.
