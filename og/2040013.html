🌶️ Air Fryer Harissa-Spiced Chicken Breast with Roasted Zucchini, Cherry Tomatoes & Quinoa

30 min · Medium · North African · 509 kcal per serving

Ingredients

  • 1.75 lbs chicken breast
  • 2 medium zucchini
  • 1.5 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 1.5 cups quinoa
  • 0.5 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 piece lemon
  • 3 tbsp harissa paste
  • 4 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil
  • 1.5 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 0.25 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 oz feta cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach

Instructions

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 400°F for 3 minutes. Meanwhile, rinse quinoa and combine with 3 cups chicken broth in a small saucepan over high heat; bring to a boil, then reduce to low, cover, and cook until liquid is absorbed and quinoa is fluffy, about 14 minutes. Remove from heat and fold in spinach, 1 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil, the juice of 1 lemon, and a pinch of black pepper; cover and set aside.
  2. Pat chicken breasts completely dry with paper towels — this is critical for a proper crust in the air fryer. Slice each breast horizontally into two even cutlets. Whisk together harissa paste, 2 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, and cinnamon in a bowl, then coat cutlets thoroughly on all sides with the marinade.
  3. Arrange chicken cutlets in a single layer in the air fryer basket — do not overlap. Air fry at 400°F for 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway at 5 minutes, until deeply caramelized on the exterior and an instant-read thermometer reads 165°F at the thickest point. Transfer to a cutting board and rest 5 minutes.
  4. Toss zucchini (cut into 1-inch half-moons) and cherry tomatoes with 1 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil, red pepper flakes, and black pepper directly in a bowl. Working in batches if needed, air fry vegetables in a single layer at 375°F for 8–10 minutes, shaking the basket once at 4 minutes, until zucchini is tender with charred edges and tomatoes are blistered and beginning to burst.
  5. Whisk Greek yogurt with the juice of the second lemon, remaining 1 tsp harissa (scraped from the bowl), and black pepper until smooth and pourable — this is your harissa-lemon drizzle sauce.
  6. Slice chicken cutlets against the grain on a bias. Divide quinoa among three bowls, top with roasted zucchini and burst tomatoes, fan sliced chicken over the top, crumble feta evenly over each bowl, and drizzle generously with harissa-lemon yogurt sauce. Taste, adjust pepper as needed, and serve immediately while the chicken is still warm.
